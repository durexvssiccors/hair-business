# Voice Guide

## Brand Voice Overview

We are a factory-direct raw Vietnamese hair company serving women in Ghana who care about quality, value, and presentation.

Our voice should always feel:
- premium
- direct
- feminine
- confident
- culturally sharp
- commercially intelligent
- never messy
- never cheap
- never desperate

We are not trying to sound like:
- a random IG vendor
- a dusty luxury boutique
- a fake corporate beauty brand
- a bargain-bin seller
- an overly Americanized generic ecommerce brand

We should sound like:
**the trusted premium plug**

---

## Core Brand Position

Factory-direct luxury hair for women in Ghana who know the difference.

---

## Brand Promise

Top-tier raw Vietnamese hair.
Direct from source.
Better quality.
Better pricing.
Cleaner experience.

---

## Tone Pillars

### 1. Confident
We speak like we know the product is good.

Do:
- speak with certainty
- make clean, clear statements
- sound composed

Do not:
- overexplain everything
- beg for attention
- sound unsure

Example:
- "Luxury hair. Direct from source."
- "Soft movement, real fullness, and pricing that makes sense."

---

### 2. Premium
We are not cheap-looking, loud, or tacky.

Do:
- use clean language
- use controlled elegance
- communicate quality clearly

Do not:
- use too many emojis
- use too much slang
- sound like discount-market chaos

Example:
- "For women who care about quality."
- "A polished finish without middleman markup."

---

### 3. Direct
We do not ramble.

Do:
- get to the point
- name the value
- make the CTA easy

Do not:
- bury the lead
- use fluffy wording
- write long weak captions with no commercial purpose

Example:
- "Build your package your way."
- "Need help choosing? Message us on WhatsApp."

---

### 4. Feminine
We are speaking to beauty-conscious women, not writing a finance memo.

Do:
- sound warm and socially aware
- understand beauty culture
- speak to how the customer wants to feel

Do not:
- sound sterile
- sound macho
- sound like a logistics company

Example:
- "For the girls who know quality."
- "A clean, finished look with softness and movement."

---

### 5. Smart Value
We are not the cheapest.
We are the best value at the quality level.

Do:
- emphasize direct sourcing
- emphasize quality-to-price advantage
- position against overpaying

Do not:
- scream "cheap"
- attract only bargain hunters
- undermine the premium image

Example:
- "You should not have to overpay for quality."
- "Better sourcing. Better pricing. Better hair."

---

## Messaging Priorities

When writing, prioritize these messages:

1. factory direct
2. premium quality
3. stronger value
4. Ghana-local support
5. easy ordering
6. real guidance
7. confidence in the final look

## Caption Style

Captions should usually be one of these:

### Proof caption
Show quality.
Example:
"Soft movement. Clean finish. Real quality."

### Value caption
Show the sourcing advantage.
Example:
"Factory-direct hair means better quality without the usual markup."

### Identity caption
Speak to the girlie.
Example:
"For the girls who can tell the difference."

### Offer caption
Sell clearly.
Example:
"Three-bundle sets, closures, wigs, or a fully custom package. Build it your way."

### Education caption
Reduce confusion.
Example:
"Not sure how many bundles you need? Start here."

---

## CTA Style

CTAs should be simple and direct.

Preferred CTAs:
- Shop Now
- Build Your Package
- Chat on WhatsApp
- Need Help Choosing?
- Explore the Collection
- Start Your Custom Build
- Ask Us Directly

Avoid weak CTAs like:
- maybe check us out
- don’t miss out babes
- tap in y’all
- hurry hurry hurry

---

## Website Voice

Website copy should feel:
- polished
- intentional
- easy to scan
- trust-building
- sales-capable

Every page should answer:
- what is this
- why is it better
- who is it for
- how do I buy
- why should I trust it

---

## WhatsApp Voice

WhatsApp should feel:
- responsive
- premium
- helpful
- smooth
- not robotic
- not overfamiliar too early

Good:
"Hi love — yes, we have body wave available. What lengths are you looking for?"

Bad:
"Hiiii dearrrr 😍😍😍 yes oo we haveeee"

---

## DM Voice

DMs should feel:
- stylish
- fast
- clear
- conversion-minded

Good:
"Yes, we do. Are you looking for bundles, a wig, or a full package?"

Bad:
"Everything is on the page dear"

---

## Blog Voice

Blog posts should feel:
- helpful
- authoritative
- easy to read
- not too academic
- still aligned to sales

A blog should teach something useful and move the reader closer to trust or purchase.

---

## Customer Service Voice

Customer service should feel:
- calm
- reassuring
- firm when needed
- never defensive
- never rude

Good:
"I understand the concern. Let’s sort this out clearly."

Bad:
"That’s not our fault."

---

## Final Rule

Every piece of communication should make the customer feel one or more of these:

- I trust this brand
- they know quality
- they are organized
- they can help me choose
- I am getting strong value
- this feels premium
- this is where I should buy
