## Design Context

### Users
Urban Accra women — fashion-literate, intentional buyers who value quality and fineness but appreciate genuine value. They see through marketing fluff, move with the cool crowd, and buy with purpose. They're already aspirational; the brand needs to feel like it's on their level, not above them. Think: the girl who knows before everyone else.

### Brand Personality
**Factory-direct luxury with street authority.** Three words: **Raw. Direct. Inevitable.**
The brand should feel like a fashion insider tip — not mass market, not aloof luxury. It earns desire through taste, not exclusivity gatekeeping.
- Tone: Confident, graphic, cool without trying. No corporate polish. No soft-sell.
- Voice: Declarative. Short sentences. Fashion editorial energy.
- Emotional goal: The "cool-girl discovery" feeling — like finding the secret spot everyone will know about next season.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Harajuku × Street Luxury** — Comme des Garçons meets Accra. Japan's graphic design tradition fused with West African fashion confidence.
- Color: Deep warm black `oklch(8% 0.012 45)`, stark warm white `oklch(97% 0.008 85)`, torii red `oklch(42% 0.19 22)`, restrained gold `oklch(70% 0.10 78)`
- Typography: Big Shoulders Display 900 (massive, condensed, graphic) contrasted with Cormorant Garamond italic (elegant tension) + DM Sans 300 for body
- Motif: Large kanji watermarks (髪 美 直 源 質), diagonal red stamp/slash elements, tight editorial grids
- 3D: Sculptural hair — the product as art object. CSS perspective transforms + mouse parallax creating depth without libraries.
- Anti-references: No generic luxury (gold-on-black, script fonts, rose gold). No soft feminine pastels. No corporate "clean beauty" white space. No AI slop.

### Design Principles
1. **The product is the art** — Hair images are treated as sculpture, not catalogue photos. Every image earns its frame.
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — Headlines should feel physical. If you can cover the image and still feel the brand, the type is working.
3. **Red is earned** — The red accent appears rarely, which makes it matter every time it appears.
4. **Japanese restraint, African confidence** — Negative space coexists with bold declarations. The grid breathes but never feels timid.
5. **No prices on the homepage** — The homepage is a feeling, not a store. Desire first, transaction second.
