# How to Tell If Your Hair Is Actually Raw (The Tests Every Ghana Buyer Should Know)

The Ghana hair market has a labelling problem. Walk into almost any hair vendor in Accra — or scroll through Instagram — and you'll see "raw," "raw Vietnamese," "100% raw," "raw unprocessed" everywhere.

Most of it is not raw.

This is not a minor technicality. Real raw hair lasts 3–5 years. Processed hair sold as raw lasts 6–12 months, sometimes less. The difference in performance — and in how your money works — is significant.

Here are the exact tests to run before and after you buy.

---

## Before You Buy: Questions to Ask Any Vendor

**1. Where does the hair come from?**

The only hair widely accepted as truly raw is Vietnamese hair, with a small amount of Cambodian hair. Indian Remy, Brazilian, and Peruvian hair are almost universally processed before export, regardless of how it's labelled.

If a vendor can't name the origin country specifically, that's a red flag.

**2. What's the price per bundle?**

This is the most reliable filter. Genuine raw Vietnamese hair in Ghana costs **$45–$90 per bundle** depending on length. The supply chain cost of sourcing, importing via air freight, and selling factory-direct simply doesn't allow for prices below that.

If a vendor is selling "raw Vietnamese hair" bundles at $20–$30 each: it is not raw Vietnamese hair.

**3. Is it single-drawn?**

Single-drawn bundles have natural taper — slightly thicker at the root and thinner at the tip, because that's how hair actually grows. Double-drawn bundles are machine-sorted for uniform thickness from root to tip. Perfectly uniform hair is a red flag — it suggests processing.

**4. Do they have a returns policy?**

A vendor who is confident in their product will have a clear return or exchange policy. No policy means they know the hair won't hold up to scrutiny.

## The Physical Tests: Before and After Purchase

### Test 1: The Feel Test (before purchase)

Run a section of dry hair through your fingers from root to tip, then tip to root.

- **Genuine raw hair:** Feels slightly different in each direction — a little natural resistance going against the cuticle. It will feel real, slightly textured, not artificially silky.
- **Processed hair:** Feels identically smooth in both directions — because the cuticle has been stripped and coated with silicone. This artificial smoothness is the silicone coating talking.

That silky-smooth feel is appealing, but it's temporary.

### Test 2: The Wet Test (most reliable, post-purchase)

Wash a small section of the hair with a standard shampoo — no conditioner yet.

**Genuine raw hair:** Dries slightly coarser initially. The natural texture (wave, straight, or curl) reasserts itself naturally with water. The hair feels real, responsive, and alive.

**Processed hair:** Dries rough, frizzy, or flat. Without the silicone coating, the underlying stripped or chemically treated hair reveals itself. This gets worse with every wash.

### Test 3: The Water Absorption Test

Drop a small strand into a glass of water.

- **Genuine raw hair:** Sinks or absorbs water relatively quickly — natural hair is porous and absorbs moisture
- **Heavily silicone-coated processed hair:** May resist absorbing water initially as the silicone repels moisture

This test is not definitive on its own but is useful in combination with the other tests.

### Test 4: The Bleach Test (for the committed buyer)

Apply a small amount of bleach to a test strand.

- **Genuine raw hair:** Lifts evenly and predictably, the same way natural hair does. No unexpected reactions.
- **Processed or treated hair:** May lift unevenly, turn unpredictable colours, or show signs of damage at the coated sections. Some processed hair reacts badly to bleach because of the chemical treatments already applied.

Only do this on hair you're prepared to sacrifice.

### Test 5: The Heat Test

Apply flat iron heat (around 180–200°C) to a small section.

- **Genuine raw hair:** Responds like natural hair — straightens cleanly, holds the style, and recovers with moisture.
- **Processed hair with silicone:** The silicone can melt slightly at high heat, creating a waxy residue or an unusual smell. The hair may also not respond correctly or may become brittle faster.

## What Good Raw Hair Feels Like Over Time

If you've been using processed hair and switch to genuine raw Vietnamese hair, the first thing most customers notice is that the hair **settles better over time**, not worse.

In the first week: slightly more natural-feeling than processed hair (less artificial silky-smooth, more real).
After a month of washing and styling: it looks and feels exactly as it did on day one — because there's no coating to wear off.
After a year: still performing, still full, still soft.

That's the raw difference.
