## What "Raw Hair" Actually Means

Real raw hair is hair that has been cut from a single donor, collected, and bundled without any chemical processing. No acid baths. No silicone coating. No steam processing to create an artificial wave pattern.

The result: hair that behaves exactly like natural hair. It can be:
- Dyed and bleached without immediate damage
- Heat-styled repeatedly without going dry or frizzy
- Washed and conditioned just like your own hair
- Worn for 3–5 years with proper care

Most of what is sold in Ghana — and across West Africa — is **remy processed hair** or **virgin processed hair**. These are treated with chemicals to look raw but will lose quality within 6–12 months.

## Raw Hair Vendors in Ghana: What to Look For

Before paying any vendor, ask these questions:

**1. Where is the hair sourced from?**
The only hair widely accepted as truly raw is Vietnamese hair and a small amount of Cambodian hair. Indian Remy and Brazilian hair are almost always chemically processed before export.

**2. Is it single-drawn or double-drawn?**
Single-drawn bundles have natural taper (thinner at the ends). Double-drawn bundles are machine-sorted for uniform thickness — a red flag for processing.

**3. Can you see the weft?**
On genuine raw hair, the weft (where the hair is sewn) will be clean and tight. Processed hair often has a chemical smell at the weft.

**4. What is the return policy?**
A vendor who is confident in their product will have a real policy. If there is no returns information, that tells you everything.

## How to Tell If Your Current Hair Is Actually Raw

Simple test: run a small section through your fingers when dry. Genuinely raw hair will feel slightly coarse at first — not silky-smooth. That silky feeling is silicone coating on processed hair. After a wash or two, truly raw hair settles into a natural texture. Processed hair starts to feel dry and rough.
