# Bone Straight vs Body Wave: Which Raw Hair Texture Is Right for You?

Two of our most popular textures. Two very different results. And the decision comes up almost every time a customer is building a new set.

Here's the honest breakdown of bone straight vs body wave raw Vietnamese hair — so you choose the texture that actually fits your lifestyle, not just the one that looks good in a photo.

---

## Bone Straight Raw Vietnamese Hair

Bone straight is exactly what it sounds like: completely straight, with no wave or bend from root to tip. In raw Vietnamese hair, this texture has a naturally slight sheen and a soft, smooth drape.

### Best for:
- Sleek, polished everyday looks
- Customers who prefer a clean, minimal aesthetic
- Installs that need to be styled quickly and consistently
- Women who flat-iron regularly — raw bone straight doesn't fight the iron
- Formal and professional settings

### Maintenance:
Low to moderate. Bone straight sits clean between styling sessions. It doesn't require re-setting between washes the way a wave pattern does.

### The raw difference:
Processed bone straight hair often feels plasticky — overly smooth from silicone, and flat after the first wash. Genuine raw bone straight has a natural, slightly weighted drape. It moves. It doesn't feel fake.

**Available in:** Bundles (12"–30"), 5x5 Closure Wigs, 13x4 Frontal Wigs, Closures, Frontals

---

## Body Wave Raw Vietnamese Hair

Body wave has a loose, natural S-wave pattern. In genuinely raw Vietnamese hair, this wave is real — created by the natural growth pattern of the donor's hair, not by steam processing.

### Best for:
- Customers who want more volume and movement
- Versatile installs — body wave can be straightened or enhanced with a wand
- Women who like the hair to have life and bounce
- Customers who prefer a more casual, effortless aesthetic
- Longer lengths where volume matters more

### Maintenance:
Moderate. Body wave needs to be refreshed after washing — a little mousse or a light setting spray brings the wave back. But on raw hair, the wave reactivates naturally with water, so upkeep is straightforward.

### The raw difference:
Processed body wave is created by steaming straight hair into a mould. The wave degrades with heat and washing. Raw Vietnamese body wave is the natural texture of the donor — which means the wave survives washing and heat cycling far better than processed alternatives.

**Available in:** Bundles (12"–30"), 5x5 Closure Wigs, 13x4 Frontal Wigs, Closures, Frontals

## Which One Should You Choose?

**Choose bone straight if:**
- You want a clean, low-effort look on most days
- You prefer smooth and polished over full and bouncy
- You flat-iron regularly
- You're going for a professional or minimal aesthetic

**Choose body wave if:**
- You want more volume and a more textured look
- You like the hair to have natural movement without styling
- You want more visual impact and drama
- You prefer versatility — body wave can be worn wavy or straightened
