# Closure vs Frontal: Which Is Right for You?

If you are building a sew-in install, one of the most common decisions is:
**Should you use a closure or a frontal?**

Both create a cleaner, more polished finish than leaving your natural hair out. The right choice comes down to the result you want and how much styling flexibility you need.

---

## What Is a Closure?

A closure is a smaller lace piece — typically **4x4** or **5x5** — placed at the crown of the head to seal the install and create a natural-looking parting.

### A 5x5 closure is the right choice if you want:
- A clean, natural everyday finish
- Lower maintenance and easier upkeep
- A structured install that holds its parting
- A smaller lace footprint that's easier to melt and blend

For most sew-in customers in Ghana, a closure is the **easiest and most practical** finishing option.

## What Is a Frontal?

A frontal is a larger lace piece — typically **13x4** — that spans the full hairline from ear to ear. It gives you far more styling flexibility because it covers the entire front perimeter of the head.

### A 13x4 frontal is the right choice if you want:
- Maximum styling freedom — slick it back, part anywhere, wear it off the face
- A more dramatic, high-glam finished look
- Versatility for different styles from the same install
- A full hairline illusion

## Which One Is Right for Your Install?

### Choose a 5x5 Closure if:
- Your length is 24 inches or under
- You want a polished daily install without high maintenance
- This is your first lace install
- You want easy upkeep week-to-week

### Choose a 13x4 Frontal if:
- You want to pull the hair back or part it anywhere
- You're building a high-impact look for an event or photoshoot
- You're experienced with lace and confident in blending
- You want maximum styling range from the same install

## How Many Bundles Do You Need with a Closure or Frontal?

A closure or frontal contributes density to the front and top of the install, which affects how many bundles you need for the back and sides.

**With a closure:** 3 bundles is the standard for most lengths up to 24 inches.
**With a frontal:** 3 bundles works for most installs; 4 bundles for 26 inches and above.
