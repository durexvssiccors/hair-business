# Objection Handling

## Purpose

This document provides clean responses to common sales objections.

The goal is not to argue with the customer.
The goal is to:
- maintain trust
- reduce uncertainty
- preserve premium positioning
- move the conversation forward

---

## Objection 1: “It’s too expensive.”

### Response
I understand.

Our focus is not being the cheapest random option in the market — it’s giving you stronger quality and better value at the level we sell.

Because we source direct, the pricing makes more sense than a lot of heavily marked-up options.

If you want, I can recommend the best fit within the range you’re comfortable with.

### Strategy
Reframe from “price” to “value.”

---

## Objection 2: “How do I know the quality is real?”

### Response
That’s a fair question.

A lot of hair is marketed as premium when it really isn’t. Our difference is that we focus on premium raw Vietnamese hair selected for softness, movement, and long-term value.

If you want, I can show you close-up videos or recommend the strongest options for the look you want.

### Strategy
Acknowledge skepticism and offer proof.

---

## Objection 3: “I’m not sure what to buy.”

### Response
No problem — that’s exactly what we help with.

If you tell me the look you want, your preferred texture, and whether you want bundles or a wig, I can guide you to the best fit.

### Strategy
Turn uncertainty into consultation.

---

## Objection 4: “I’m just looking.”

### Response
Of course.

If it helps, I can narrow things down for you by texture, length, or style so you don’t have to sort through everything yourself.

### Strategy
Keep the door open without pressure.

---

## Objection 5: “I need to think about it.”

### Response
Absolutely.

If you want, I can save the recommendation I gave you and help you pick it back up whenever you’re ready.

### Strategy
Reduce pressure while preserving continuity.

---

## Objection 6: “Do I really need that many bundles?”

### Response
For most full installs, 3 bundles works really well.

If you want a more natural look, you may need less. If you want extra fullness or longer lengths, you may want more.

I can recommend the right quantity based on the exact finish you want.

### Strategy
Educate and personalize.

---

## Objection 7: “What’s the difference between closure and frontal?”

### Response
A closure gives you a cleaner, more natural finish with less maintenance.

A frontal gives you more styling flexibility and a more dramatic final look.

If you tell me the style you want, I can recommend the better option for you.

### Strategy
Clarify and guide.

---

## Objection 8: “Why should I buy from you instead of someone else?”

### Response
Our edge is simple:
- premium raw Vietnamese hair
- direct-from-source value
- a cleaner buying experience
- guidance that helps you choose properly

We focus on giving customers quality, clarity, and better pricing logic instead of confusion and overmarking.

### Strategy
Summarize the edge without sounding defensive.

---

## Objection 9: “Can I order just one item?”

### Response
Yes, absolutely.

You can order one item or build a full package depending on what you need.

### Strategy
Remove false barriers.

---

## Objection 10: “Can I mix lengths or order multiple items?”

### Response
Yes.

You can build a custom package with mixed lengths, mixed categories, and multiple quantities. If you want, I can help you structure the exact package.

### Strategy
Push Custom Build.

---

## Objection 11: “I want the best option.”

### Response
Perfect.

If you want the strongest recommendation without compromising, I can guide you toward the premium option that best fits the look you’re going for.

### Strategy
Move high-intent buyer into premium lane.

---

## Objection 12: “I want something in my budget.”

### Response
Of course.

If you tell me the range you want to stay within, I’ll recommend the best fit without wasting your time.

### Strategy
Keep the sale alive without cheapening the brand.

---

## Objection 13: “I don’t want to make the wrong choice.”

### Response
That makes sense.

Tell me the look you want, and I’ll narrow it down to the best option so you can choose with more confidence.

### Strategy
Position the brand as guide, not pressure seller.

---

## Final Rule

Objection handling should do four things:

1. acknowledge the concern
2. reinforce trust
3. clarify the value
4. move the customer forward
