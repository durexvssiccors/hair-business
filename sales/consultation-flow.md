# Consultation Flow

## Purpose

This flow helps guide customers toward the right product based on their look, preferences, and level of certainty.

The goal is not just to answer questions.
The goal is to help the customer choose and buy confidently.

---

## Consultation Objectives

We want to learn:
- what look the customer wants
- what type of product she wants
- how much guidance she needs
- what level of spend is realistic
- how quickly she wants to move

---

## Step 1: Identify Product Format

Ask:
Are you looking for:
- bundles
- a wig
- a full package
- a custom order

Goal:
Reduce confusion immediately.

---

## Step 2: Identify Desired Look

Ask:
What kind of look are you going for?

Examples:
- everyday polished
- soft glam
- fuller statement look
- event-ready
- birthday/wedding/trip look

Goal:
Understand emotional and visual target.

---

## Step 3: Identify Texture Preference

Ask:
Do you already know what texture you want?

Examples:
- bone straight
- body wave
- deep wave
- curly

If not:
recommend based on desired look.

Goal:
Match product to style intention.

---

## Step 4: Identify Length Preference

Ask:
What length range are you thinking about?

Examples:
- shorter and easy
- medium and versatile
- long and dramatic

Goal:
Guide the scale of the order.

---

## Step 5: Identify Quantity / Fullness Need

Ask:
Do you want a lighter natural look or a fuller finish?

Goal:
Determine bundle count or unit recommendation.

---

## Step 6: Identify Occasion

Ask:
Is this for:
- everyday wear
- birthday
- wedding
- travel
- special event
- general luxury upgrade

Goal:
Understand urgency and visual stakes.

---

## Step 7: Identify Budget Comfort Zone

Ask:
Do you want the best fit regardless, or would you like me to guide you within a certain range?

Goal:
Keep the conversation commercial without sounding harsh.

---

## Step 8: Present Recommendation

Response structure:
1. recommended product
2. why it fits
3. quantity
4. optional add-ons
5. next action

Template:
Based on what you want, I’d recommend [PRODUCT].

Why this fits:
- [reason 1]
- [reason 2]
- [reason 3]

For the best result, I’d suggest:
- [quantity]
- [optional closure/frontal/wig/add-on]

Would you like to go ahead with this option, or do you want me to suggest a second option too?

---

## Step 9: Branch by Customer Type

### If customer is confident
Move to close.

### If customer is uncertain
Offer a second recommendation.

### If customer wants full control
Move to Custom Build.

### If customer is price-sensitive
Offer best-fit lower-friction option without sounding cheap.

---

## Recommendation Logic Examples

### Customer wants polished everyday look
Recommend:
- body wave or straight
- core bundle set
- closure if she wants clean finish

### Customer wants birthday or statement look
Recommend:
- elite bundle or wig
- longer lengths
- optional frontal

### Customer wants convenience
Recommend:
- wig

### Customer wants exact control
Recommend:
- custom build

### Customer is a stylist or bulk buyer
Recommend:
- custom build
- mixed quantity order

---

## Closing Options

### Option 1
Would you like to place the order?

### Option 2
Would you like a second recommendation first?

### Option 3
Would you like to build a custom package instead?

---

## Consultation Rule

Do not overwhelm the customer with too many choices too early.

Guide.
Narrow.
Recommend.
Close.
